# Distinguish new rule suggestions from established process state

## Requirements

- A **“rule suggestions”** section should contain only **new** governance improvements, tightening proposals, or not-yet-landed follow-ups.
- Existing baseline sync mechanics, standing review flows, and descriptions of rules already in force are **process state / continuation context**, not new suggestions.
- Content that only says where the next baseline starts, that a topic finished, or how work continues normally belongs in **general progress**, not under rule suggestions.
- Elevate content to a rule suggestion only when the **process itself** changes (stricter, looser, or new boundary).

## Why this exists

Mixing “new improvements” with “we followed the usual flow” dilutes real governance items and inflates review cost.

## Local tuning

- How local stop reports or reviews separate “suggestions” vs “status.”  
- What is baseline mechanics vs a new proposal.  
- Whether follow-up topics should drop items that are merely completed process.

## How to verify

- Suggestion sections only hold new or pending governance items.  
- Established mechanics and completed topics appear as ordinary status, not new rules.  
- Review topic lists focus on deltas worth local judgment, not restating existing process.
